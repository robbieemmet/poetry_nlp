Bear Country

It's not important that you see it,
but rather that you feel it
or you see that it is there,

the torn-up earth,
the scarred piles of leavings.

It is important that you know
what would happen if you cross it.

It knows how to swim and climb and wait
and winter is to its advantage.

It eats anything and everything.
It knows no boundaries -
it sees all places as its territory.
