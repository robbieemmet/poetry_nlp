Tweety Bird

For JoAnn Wich

I really should sing this.

“The beauty of the sound
comes from the generosity of the heart”
and I remember
the recording from your opera career,
how you left that behind
to give everything
to musical education and to your family.

I don't just mean
your wonderful husband and kids -
you were the Junior School grandma,
so warm and kind to timid seventh-graders,
so gentle and coaxing of shy and changing voices.

I can see why you had us memorize our music.
That way you could look into our eyes,
find our souls and tell them to shine.
You gave us your talent and your love
and I hope we gave enough back.

I will remember you
when I see angels on trees,
when I hear John Rutter’s carols
or birdsong on the breeze.

I will remember you
whenever I think of Hop.
I will remember you when I sing
“High Up On The Mountain Top.”
