Chill Night

On countless stoves, a thousand kettles sing.
This is a night for fire and food and tea.
The doors are shut. The kith are wintering,
alone or in some small community:
a lively tavern with the local mice
eating their seeds and drinking warming brews,
two otters skating on the moonlit ice,
a solitary bear deep in his snooze.
This is a night to watch the roaring flame,
to make a blanket fort inside your home
and thank each small forgotten god by name
that you have settled here, no more to roam.
What life is there without a chilly night,
some darkness to remind you of the light?
