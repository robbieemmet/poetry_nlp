The Gateling Dry

The heat has peaked. The fields replete with hay
lie baking in a warm and gentle sun.
There isn't enough time in the short day
to close out Breathe with a last burst of fun.
Not that it's not fun to enjoy the stars
when they come earlier on frosty nights,
out by a bonfire with lutes and guitars,
surrounded by the fireflies’ last lights.
But there is something special in the rush
to squeeze the pulp out of the shrinking glow,
each day drier and shorter, but still lush,
a last great shout before the time of snow.
Reflect, but still have fun. Look at the sky.
Enjoy the fleeting cooling Gateling dry.
