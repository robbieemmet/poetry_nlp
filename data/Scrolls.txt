Scrolls

Where there is no life
there is no death.

Where there is no death
there is no life.

Water is life, they say,
so water is also decay

and change and death.
Because the desert is so devoid of us,

that is also where our prints remain,
changed by wind and sand but not by rot,

those scrolls that have captured
so much of what we know

of certain histories.
And there are different truths

in sitting down and saying,
“This is how things happened and I will tell you why”,

and in writing simply,
“I miss you. Come home.”

or “I bought three boatfuls of lentils and a donkey.”

Even in what is written
what we say is not what we do.

I try to be who I want to be,
but I too am caught in the end by scrolls.

Even the news -
how is it news

if how I consume it
is always the same?
