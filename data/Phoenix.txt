Phoenix

Dark desert highways glow with burning light -
the city never sleeps, there is no dawn,
for all the woods that gave the feel of night
are burned up with the houses - both are gone.
You stay in a hotel. You don't know when
or if you'll go back home, you only know
there was a place that you called home back then,
before you came to no place else to go.
Maybe the phoenix is the wind of wings
that fans the flames and burns the houses down,
the lives material, the gathered things
that make a home, a street, a block, a town.
Maybe the phoenix is the road through pain
to gather ash and give it life again.
