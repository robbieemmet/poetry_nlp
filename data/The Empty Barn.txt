The Empty Barn

It's cold now.
The dairy cows
used to keep it warm
in winter,
the sweet laugh of hay,
sharp chuckle
of manure and silage
deafening the nose.

Inside the barn
there was certainty:
the cows needed to be milked
twice a day.
Outside the barn
farms grew and others folded,
milk never quite
stopped tasting greasy on your tongue.

Things come and go.
I wish it were any easier
when those things were how we saw the world -
the view from the hay loft
or the empty barn's door.
