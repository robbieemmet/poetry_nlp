Draft

Tear holes in the thin layer
over the windows to my hole.

I gasp at the cold
and try to burrow deeper,

but the earth turns and the wind
tunnels over my head.

When you come of age you are a seed - 
you have to be buried

to live again.
And you can't see your roots

and the opportunities, the perfect strangers
you can choose to trust or signal out against.

Then you gasp and stretch
and grasp the sky and you are reaped,

a dusty harvest to be turned
into the earth again.

But all there is is growing pain,
or crumpling up the earth to start again,

soil pressed by the wind, paper-thin,
into a blank slate.
