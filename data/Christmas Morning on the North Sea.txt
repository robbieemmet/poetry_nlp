Christmas Morning on the North Sea

This morning, crisp and clear,
the spice of brine and bladderwrack,
this darkest time of year.

The sea's a bluish-black,
except for white tips on the swells.
Nobody can go back

out on the deep and frozen hells
that roar with ancient, tongueless sound,
competing with the bells

of the small coastal town,
bedecked with holly, ice, and spray.
What sweetness I have found
here on this bitter winter day.
