The Last Story Told on Rhen Var

There was, at first, a rumbling in the ground,
and smoke poured from a mountain north a ways.
The villages unsettled by the sound
had worse to come - a cooling of the days.
The villagers had planted Birren grain,
but after sprouting, soon it failed to grow.
The fields were turned to marshes by the rain,
and too soon after rain, there followed snow.
The sun was black, the gray sky gave no heat.
Both peasant shacks and palaces went cold.
In time, there was not even grass to eat,
and that was the last story ever told.
The living fled the world in panicked waves.
The dead still tell this story from their graves.
