Vigil

Night falls,
and I think it’s the darkest yet.
No fireflies even in this early summer,
when the air is supposed to be full
of what could be.

Instead there are only questions,
grief, what could have been,
anxiety.
The darkness grins and promises
good things to bad people.

I used to think bad people
didn't wake up early.
Now I wake up early
not because I'm some “good” Puritan,
but because

the siren call of early death,
not wanted but always
a possibility,
haunts both dusk and dawn,
prowling the camps and fire-rings

of my mind,
taking hope from day
and adding fear to night.
I cannot think
the last time I saw light.
