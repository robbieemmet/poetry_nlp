Flowers

The hurricane bloomed overnight,
a god of wind erupting like a flower.
This is how a world will end again.

Or how many worlds, uncountable,
will constrict in stagnant water,
isolated, shrinking,
like fingers in a bath too long?

How could such quiet be so wrong?

No blooms next spring
except the pioneers and volunteers,
taking their names from humans who gave up
their titles to the wild.

No limits, no means,
no distribution, no probability,

only the worthless predictions
of a waterlogged palm.
