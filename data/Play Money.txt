Play Money

In my mind I walk into
my school's library
with plastic coins,
ready to buy potential presents -
at that time, my futures as I saw them.

The present was right there all along.

An armchair,
and a window to dream out of.
It only takes one object
to tell a story -

a hatchet,
a bush plane,
half a canoe.

A book can carry these
and more.

I get the idea of the Bible, I really do -
those books of wilderness
and braver people
can be my compass points.

And I pay dearly
for their dreams to be mine.

Not to be there -
it doesn't matter that I'm there.
It matter that it’s there,

that even if I never see a loon
or the northern lights,

I can feel the North
in the last bite of winter,

in the rustle of leaves
in books and trees,

and when I turn the last page
and leave behind the plastic hoard
I've piled up like a dragon,

I or my atoms or my dreams
can be there
because it still exists

and so, beyond myself,
do I.
