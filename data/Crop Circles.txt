Crop Circles

The maize towers high above you.
Use your ears to guide you.

How long, the silky whispers come and say,
how long has it been

since you traced a labyrinth,
not lost but wandering,

into its center?
Life thrives on edges,

and always you existed
on the cusp of field and forest,

the wolf in the back of your mind
and the apple in your eye.

Now you have your cider and you have your dreams,
the years of labor leading you

to tended fields and clean and bountiful streams
and in your mind the vision of something greater,

balance and promise, gift and compact,
the settled and the wild coexisting.

You have come from stock to be proud of
and planted hopeful seeds.

Graft yourself onto the giant shoulders
of this wealth of apple trees.
