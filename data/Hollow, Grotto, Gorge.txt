Hollow, Grotto, Gorge

This is the price
of life in the mountains -
the sunset comes

always too early, I think,
sun sinking too soon
below the blocked horizon,

and in each hollow,
shadows grow long and multiply
like subdividing cells.

I much prefer
the broad face of the river,
that warm, abundant grace

of memory of sandbar picnics,
minor snags and secret grottoes,
which I would sooner die than have erased.

We come, at last, back up into the mountains.
The river's voice has gravel, at its head.
What life we have, we sunset-colored salmon.
What life we lay down in the riverbed.
