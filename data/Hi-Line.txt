Hi-Line

One of those cattle-killing blizzards
rips up snow to blow and drift again.
Holed up in Seattle,
I dream through the rain
of witnessing that power
from a powerless train.

The blizzard whips up stubble
left by amber waves of grain.
Wolf Point, Havre, others
pray for blue skies in vain.
The train and I are siloed in Seattle
but in my mind I take that line
across that greatest plain.
