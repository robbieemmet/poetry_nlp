Tents

One time at sleepaway camp
I was encouraged to eat a banana
to keep the mosquitoes away.

I was given the option of liver,
but took my chances with the
less powerful banana.

The security I felt
meant nothing itched.
In my mind I had carved a space

to be brave,
to not fear eating liver
or my liver failing,

a space where the tent walls
kept out the rabid dogs of my dreams,
where one banana

for just one day
kept the mosquitoes away.
Like the banana, that space

is eaten quickly - 
it is easy to find
but hard to keep.
