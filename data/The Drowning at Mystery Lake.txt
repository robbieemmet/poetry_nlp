The Drowning at Mystery Lake

The legend lives on in many a small town
of the big lake they call Lac Myst×re
At the lake it is said you can hear the long-dead
if you stand on the frozen shore there
The camp was alive like a busy bee hive
with many a group on vacation
For two groups that day they shouldn't have stayed
in what would be their last destination

The day was bright and the group thought they might
rent canoes and go fishing and rowing
When you do, if you're smart, and you're going to start
you should tell someone else where you're going
When the group set out, they had no shadow of a doubt
that the day would be sunny and balmy
They should have known even in summer it snows
and the lake she gets restless and stormy

They rowed and they fished, they picked cattails and wished
on the fluff that they blew from their fingers
but the wishes they blew sadly didn't come true
and they came to wish they hadn't lingered
The sky turned dark as they tried to embark
on the journey to camp and to safety
they rowed and they rowed, but the breeze made them slow
and they saw the storm rolling in quickly

They swore and they prayed, and they wished that they stayed
on the docks near the cabin they rented
The clouds they hung low, the wind started to blow
and the storm front looked wild and demented
And when the storm hit they just wanted to quit
but in spite of the odds, they kept rowing
Once it started to rain, they were not seen again
and they couldn't see where they were going

Does anyone know where the love of God goes
when the waves turn to wolves and devour?
The searchers all say they'd have been safe that day
if they'd made it to shoulder-deep water
When the storm struck, they might have been sucked
into whirlpools or dangerous eddies
there was no way to know how they died in the blow
when the searchers recovered their bodies

Great Bear roars, and the Raven Falls sing
in the depths of their perilous canyon
On the island they say you can't trust a calm day
and there's no guarantee of smooth sailing
And the rivers and lakes are like dangerous snakes
coiling over and under each other
Even on summer days they have taken away
many children and fathers and mothers

Ten bodies they found in the darkness profound
two families, three generations
There were none found alive and they couldn't be revived
in spite of the best ministrations
The legend lives on in many a small town
of the big lake they call Lac Myst×re
At the lake it is said you can hear the long-dead
if you stand on the frozen shore there
