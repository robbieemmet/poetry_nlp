Solin, a Cornbread God

Happy, his little feet run,
leaving a trail of crumbs
away from the kith asleep
under the midday sun.

He runs throughout the corn,
singing its bright ears full.
He watches the harvest come in
when the woolly bumbles are shorn.

He runs around the quern
grinding the grain into meal,
spinning around in a circle
as the bright seasons turn.

He runs around the tin,
perched on the flames and full
of the gold of fields and sunny days
with the small god's joy mixed in.

He runs around the plate,
reveling in the golden bread
spread with honey and aphid butter
that he helped create.
