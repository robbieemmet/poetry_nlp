The Mouth of the Tyne

I will arise and go now, and go to the mouth of the Tyne,
and watch the sand go dancing along the windswept shore,
and hear the seabirds crying, and watch the cold sun shine,
and wish for this and nothing more.

And I will come alive there - the wind will sharpen me,
as it has done to others, who watch the rolling wave
and smell the stinging salt-air above the dark North Sea,
a view for the weak and a pool for the brave.
