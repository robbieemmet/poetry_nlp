Campy

It's not about looks,
not in the dark
and the lingering heat of the night
and the buzz of insects

and the whisper of romances
carried out on cabin porches
and in the camp dining hall.

It's about youth,
about self-confidence
and a summer too short
to bother with trust or caution,

about the way hair looks
after a swim or shower,
how eyes look
across a campfire.

But that's camp,
fleeting -
nobody with you, not really,
but the idea.
