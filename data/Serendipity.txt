Serendipity

Garrett rifled through the cabinet,
his rifle over his shoulder.

He hadn't eaten more than rabbit
in a while.

At night he dreamed of cans of peaches.
Peach pie he almost didn't dare to dream of.

A salt shaker. Not bad.
Some salt for the next deer he shot.

He reached into the dark corner
and pulled out a small brown tin.

“I don't believe it”, he breathed.
Porcupine Drinking Chocolate.

Was he hallucinating?
Was his mind easing his death

with memories from childhood,
the long-forgotten winter days

when he sat drinking Porcupine?
He blinked and rubbed his eyes.

The chocolate stayed.
“Today is going to be a great day.”
