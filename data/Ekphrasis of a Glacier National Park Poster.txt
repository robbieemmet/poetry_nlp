Ekphrasis of a Glacier National Park Poster

They got it wrong. They got it very wrong,
for just because there's snow, that doesn't mean
the park's a snow globe. It changed all along,
changes both monumental and unseen.
The glaciers moved like sandworms. In their throats,
hundreds of lakes were born and boulders died,
and to this day, the lofty mountain goats
survey the lakes and scree left by their stride.
But just because a change is big, it's not
the end of change, however small or slow.
Water still carves the mountains when it's hot,
even if soon the summers have no snow.
No matter how much Glacier Park might change,
treating it like a poster would be strange.
