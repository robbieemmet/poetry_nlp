The Bear Gale

There is a fall I know that roars like thunder.
There is a spring I know that gently weeps.
I know no water that is named for summer.
I know the one for winter, and it's deep.

The village crouches down behind the castle.
The cliffs beneath are stern from ancient war.
They frown at vessel after shipwrecked vessel.
They raise a curled fist to the ocean's roar.

I see a line of turbines in the ocean.
How dare we steal the might of wind and wave?
The bear gale wakes up from its hibernation.
It tries to sink the turbines in their grave.

The North Sea is a winter like no other.
It is a gift to worship one so old.
So bare yourself. Embrace it like a lover.
Kiss the great snapping waves. Bear hug the cold.
