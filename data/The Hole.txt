The Hole

This place is not familiar to me.
Where are the maples?
Where are the spruce and hemlocks,
the beach plums
and the moose and the loon?

I always dreamed of the North
and until now it was here
in my New England.

I think maybe I was wrong.
Somewhere at the end of the world

where the water turns to land
once again

and the sun takes months to rise,
maybe there is a hole

and out of it the meaning
in the world is draining.

Trees, somehow, up here,
maples not where I would want them,

but Virginia bluebells and cherry blossoms
usher in a spring without a winter,

and palm trees in DC
bask in the heat of entropy,

the melting of a country
that does not know itself

though then again
maybe it never did.
