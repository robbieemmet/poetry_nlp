The Knockdown Freeze

The trees that didn't shatter in the freeze
are eaten clean by hungry woolly bears,
and any last surviving, hardy trees
are witness to a thousand cold nightmares:
kith around campfires, boiling grass and bark,
an early harvest, ruined grain and tears,
and hungry scorpion packs in the dark,
igniting all the starving people's fears.
There is still hope, roaming around at night,
a small deer god, with fangs and silver eyes,
whose luminous antlers are just so bright
they look to be a fortunate sunrise -
a sign of warmth, and harvests yet to come,
sweet pink wildflowers, and the bumbles’ hum.
