Looting the Milton Bookstore

I found the odd survival manual,
a sewing book, and Wilderness Kitchen,
out on the road. But this was something else,
a hole in time to see the world before.
Not that the store itself was in good shape,
but it had not been looted, or not yet.
Somehow they died or left before they thought
to burn the books inside, or else they felt
a sanctity they couldn't feel in church,
not when this “God” had let the lights go out.
No, this was something else. A place for wants
as well as needs, a room of time machines,
maybe the only gadgets that still worked.
Would they be worth the weight to carry out?
A book of poetry about the North?
A cookbook calling for a leg of lamb?
A history of anything before?
I had to think that they would have some worth.
Why else should I go on? Not just to learn
survival, but appreciate the sound
of words rhyming with words, even not shared,
or some reminder of the way things were,
maybe, some day, the way that things could be.
I loaded up with books, and left a tin
of Big Moose Kicks Coffee. If anyone
survived and saw that I had stolen books,
I hoped that they would think the trade was fair.
