Great Bear Passage

[Inspired by Stan Rogers’ “Northwest Passage”]

Ah, for just one time
I would take the Great Bear Passage
to find the Winding River
racing towards the frozen sea
Will I ever find
in the mountains tall and savage
the storied Great Bear Passage to the sea?

South of the great mountain
‘Tis there ‘twas said to lie
The place called Pleasant Valley
in which so many died
Seeking loot and shelter
from the ever-blowing gales
leaving nothing there but sad unfinished tales?

Ah, for just one time
I would take the Great Bear Passage
to find the Winding River
racing towards the frozen sea
Will I ever find
in the mountains tall and savage
the storied Great Bear Passage to the sea?

Not long after the lights went out
I took the rugged trail
following the others
who had tried the passage and failed
Watching barns arise before me
and the hay stacked up like gold
This broken last survivor
trying hard to beat the cold

Ah, for just one time
I would take the Great Bear Passage
to find the Winding River
racing towards the frozen sea
Will I ever find
in the mountains tall and savage
the storied Great Bear Passage to the sea?

Through the night I wandered
under strange and haunting lights,
praying for the others
who had faced the freezing nights
and broken through the mountains
laying out this path for me
to find at last salvation by the sea

Ah, for just one time
I would take the Great Bear Passage
to find the Winding River
racing towards the frozen sea
Will I ever find
in the mountains tall and savage
the storied Great Bear Passage to the sea?

What then do I owe others
who might happen on this way?
If I should leave a note for them,
what should the message say?
“I hope you can survive here
and escape this frozen hell.
If you find this note, I only wish you well.”

Ah, for just one time
I would take the Great Bear Passage
to find the Winding River
racing towards the frozen sea
Will I ever find
in the mountains tall and savage
the storied Great Bear Passage to the sea?
