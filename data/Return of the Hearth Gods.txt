Return of the Hearth Gods

Long after war linger
light-holes filling nighttimes.
Blades are melted, boiling,
breaking into day-things:
pots and pans, sing-waters,
partly black still, heart-spears
yet melded in metal,
mail chains in stair railings.

Look to the woods, wherein
wind-faces are waiting
for fire-doors open,
full and holy, ready
now for the new hearth gods,
no longer cold-sleepers,
kettle- and key-keepers,
killing none but war-guests.
