Shadows in the Valley of Death

As the evening shadows lengthen,
everything sharpens.
The mountain pointedly
shows who rules.
Even so high as this,
a man's home
is literally his castle:
even the mountains
don't make good fences
for a bad neighborhood.
How does hospitality
not curdle like cheese
or rot like liquor?
Even the shadows
and flashing eyes
are armed with knives
and water lays thick
in time-arresting snow
but blood is thicker
in the scratched faces
of fields broken
below the mountain faces.
How is there violence
in so many shadows and corners
and mountains and valleys,
in so many places?
