New Year

Bright upon Millers’ shores the first last light.
Cold upon lips the mist of warmer water
spiraling into the day pursued by night.
Another year, another degree hotter.

Taste the cool warming springs, water and season,
phasing and changing in unnatural ways,
making the maples wake up without reason,
paying with sour sap for sleepless days.

Dark is the river splitting the sun and mountains,
but for the flash of musket and of blade.
This was a land of endless fish and fountains.
This Millers River, what sort of price was paid?

Dark upon light upon dark upon light in waves.
Look in the river, count up the unmarked graves.
