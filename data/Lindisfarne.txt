Lindisfarne

The sea-pulse sings
over tracks through the trade-way.
The sea-pulse protects
as it re-opens raid-roads.
No feet over salt-fields
can come to the island
when the sea-pulse is surging,
but sea-wagons sail up
when the moon is mead-bright.
The first futhark-carvers
have traded their town-names
for eerier epithets:
slave-taker, town-baker,
cross-breaker, name-maker.
They plundered the place-names
and put down their prize-marks:
horse-bay, long-clearing
willow-farm, white-farm.
Waves come and waves go,
but wave-tracks stay in sand.
