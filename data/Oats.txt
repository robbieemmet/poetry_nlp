Oats

It wouldn't surprise me to learn
that oats are rolled flat
by the winds of the places they grow in.

They taste like
the lobster of the plains
and coastal cliffside crofts:

they taste like
whatever you put on them.
That flexibility is, itself, a gift.

Put them in the fridge overnight
if you want:
they're used to cold and dark places.

Here's an ode to the oat:
you have my heart,
you and the hard places you grow.
