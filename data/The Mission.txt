The Mission

What can you do to the random gods but pray
that you found the right farmhouse by chance,
the one your older order friends remember,
where soil turns the seasons in a dance?

Death and decay, the instruments of eruption,
play whispered snake-charms for the coiled root
and it emerges, eager for disruption,
waiting for planted chaos to bear fruit.

So quietly, the plant shoots and unravels,
unstable from the start until the end.
You weed, you water, as your faith directs you,
but you can’t break soil, you just bend.

Your mission failed. The garden burns around you,
stochastically, the crickets gently cheep
while the wild world, triumphant, turns around you,
the wolf encircling you, the foolish sheep

who thought you could contain the rambling stories
and pilgrimages buried in the seed.
Give up the plow, and with it, all your worries.
Surrender to the chaos garden’s creed.
