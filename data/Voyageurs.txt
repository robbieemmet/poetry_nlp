Voyageurs

When I think of this
mill city,
I begin to understand -

who here doesn't know
the power of water?

So many times the Acre
has flooded and emptied out,
people carried over seas
and on rivers.

So many times the city's fortunes
have risen and fallen -
no part can stay the same.

But there's a part
that knows the river
better than most others -

they were some of the first
from other shores to dare
the early winters and raging waters
in this strange land.

No renewal can raze them,
no resurrection faze them
after the one that they hold dear.

When the mills all fall away
into the Gulf of Maine,
they will still be here.

Maybe we will all speak French
some day.
