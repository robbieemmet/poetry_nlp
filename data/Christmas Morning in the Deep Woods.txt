Christmas Morning in the Deep Woods

The sun lights up the cooling earth,
the first ever midwinter's day,
and deep time spins the years away
so that we stand here and survey
that first dark year's rebirth.

The woods are too deep for the sound
of revelry to penetrate,
the ways the young ones celebrate.
They stand in almost the same state
as when they first were found.

They do not mark the passing time
with building of a thousand fires,
the gift of voices raised in choirs,
the rise and fall of roofs and spires.
They grow their rings and climb.

They are the first place of relief,
a haven for the chickadees
who prosper through the deepest freeze
with no more shelter than the trees
without a single leaf.

What better place to greet the year?
Each tree's a church, each bird's a bell.
They chime throughout the sacred dell
that dawn has come and all is well.
I'm grateful to be here.
