New Year Syrup Stomp

Trees and sleepy bugs awake
from their hibernation.
Running is the way to join
in this celebration.

Grab the buckets and the taps.
Hitch up the beetle sled.
Light the fire under the pan
in the sugar shed.

Running sap and running crews
race against each other.
Everybody pitches in,
neighbor, stranger, brother.

The syrup thickens up all day
as sunlight melts the snow.
That night, the runners gather round
the fire’s inviting glow.

Stomp your feet and clap your hands,
sing a merry song,
for Leap is here and syrup's sweet
after a Chill so long.

The next day our hearts are full
and our heads are sore,
but let's all come again next year
and run a little more.
