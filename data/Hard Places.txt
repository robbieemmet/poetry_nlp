Hard Places

The dzud snowed in every mountain on earth,

killing the livestock with the fierceness of wolves

and the swiftness of deer.

There is no spring in the steppe,
no grass in the highlands this year.

On every north-facing cliff,
every burrowing seabird
sits on an empty nest.

Every albatross
swings between craggy islands
and endless storms,
aloft without rest.

No wonder that in these places,
every heart is stone,
every hearth has a dung fire
with a simmering broth
of grass and bones.

The wind is a knife,
whetting itself
against these hard places.
Starving as the hard people
who pray for salvation,
it screeches and groans.
