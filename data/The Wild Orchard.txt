The Wild Orchard

From Erie up to Ellesmere,
from the Yukon to Cape Spear,
I never knew so fine a crop
of apples as grew here.

From the blossoms of the spring
to that rich last fall,
I think that might have been the greatest
apple crop of all.

Now in Pleasant Valley,
a rose hip is a meal.
You cannot reap, you cannot sow,
just trap and fish and steal.

Now the brambles and the snow
have buried the golden grain.
I'm not sure an apple crop
will ever grow again.

But some day some new pioneer
will bury the apple trees too.
Look at the churches, forts, and schools -
here graves are nothing new.
