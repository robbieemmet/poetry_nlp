Arbeia

I miss the taste of dates.
The other soldiers here eat pork,
something my homeland hates.
The summer here is cold and dark.
I'm stationed in a land so stark
it's like a tree stripped bare of bark.
And there my homeland waits,

across a sea of doubt.
The emperor has built a wall
and we're the last redoubt.
I doubt we'll save a thing at all,
here on this cliff before a fall.
Whatever we have wherewithal
to do, we'll do without.

~

I see four colors: green and gray
and brown and blue, out on patrol.
I'd like to sail a ship away
from here, but hungry breakers roll

and keep us soldiers on the brink
between hard people and deep place.
If I'm stuck here, at least, I think,
I'd like to see a friendly face,

but people here are far too close
to death to spare a joke or smile -
both village and our small outpost,
hard villager or hard exile.

~

“Sand dancer” - what they call the folk
who call this hellhole home.
They dance out to the ships that broke
amidst the surging foam.

They dance before they make a raid.
They dance after they loot.
They dance to show they're not afraid
of the sound of Roman boots.

I used to dance in different ways,
back in my sweet homeland.
Now, in this place of darkened days,
I dance on different sand.

~

Has no one warned you young recruits
the toll this posting takes,
the way it soaks through leather boots,
the southern minds it breaks?

I could have warned you, but you are new,
and the Romans need you too.

Oh you will wear the heavy armor
of the short and dreary days,
wish that you were home and warmer,
wish that you could leave this place.

I could have warned you of this post,
but I'm already a ghost.
