The Chanting Lake

There's a lake somewhere, I know,
overlooking the world below
from the mountains, where the snow
gleams on every ridge.

Seekers walk three times around.
At each lighthouse they will sound
a bell and let its toll resound,
a wondrous pilgrimage.

Hear the chanting in the swell,
the echo of a sacred bell,
the waters swallowing each knell,
silence in its wake.

Its sanctity I think is true,
with heaven reflected in its blue.
Let us walk now, me and you,
around the Chanting Lake.
