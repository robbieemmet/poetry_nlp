Some Views of the North Sea

I. 

After looking at it,
I know the difference between an invitation
and a summons.

II.

Up high, sharp cliffs.
Down low, mud flats.
Waves hammer the strands
and the winds sing among the sands.

The waves flash their teeth
white like keys.
The violent tattoo of these hammers
echoes on the breeze.

III.

Sometimes there is peace,
the distant sigh of a king
who has already feasted.

IV.

It roams like a hungry sea-lord,
rapping at doors and dams and dikes.
It mounts the bows of ships on rocky spikes.
The lucky nations have cliffs -

by the time they have to let it in,
they’ll have bigger things
even than the sea
to worry about.

V.

Sure, it smiles.
When the swimmers yelp and stiffen
even in the balm of summer,
it grins through their gritted teeth.
