Headlands

The Old Man in the Mountain's crumbled face
was eaten by the local omnivore -
the wind and water engineering place
in ways no god could manage anymore.
The signs are everywhere - the torn-up roots
of ground-down teeth, once-mighty mountain peaks
now weathered more by mountain-climbing routes
and worn smooth by the winter-swollen creeks.
The mountains high, the night-dark sea beneath.
So different, but in either, if you came,
the pressure shatters noses, eyes, and teeth,
and swallows you in darkness just the same.
The wind and water are a hungry bear:
no god could save you from the power there.
